Air

"Air pollution refers to the presence of harmful substances in the Earth's atmosphere, which can have adverse effects on human health, the environment, and overall quality of life. Air pollution can lead to various health problems, including respiratory and cardiovascular issues, as well as an increased risk of lung diseases and cancer. Additionally, it can harm ecosystems, reduce crop yields, and contribute to climate change.
Cambodia, in particular, has been increasingly concerned about air pollution, especially in urban areas, notably in the capital city, Phnom Penh. As of 2021, here are some key points regarding air pollution in Cambodia. In 2018, carbon dioxide emissions had increased by 269% compared to the year 2000, reaching a total of 11,160kt.
1. Particulate Matter (PM) Pollution: The levels of particulate matter in the air, measured as PM2.5 and PM10, have been a significant concern, particularly during specific periods throughout the year. These particles can have negative health impacts when inhaled and are often associated with vehicle emissions, construction activities, and other sources.
2. Vehicle Emissions: The increase in vehicles, especially motorcycles and tuk-tuks, has contributed to air pollution in urban areas like Phnom Penh. Many of these vehicles are older and do not meet the latest exhaust emission standards.
3. Industrial Activities: Industrial processes, power generation, and construction projects can release pollutants into the air, leading to localized air pollution.
4. Indoor Air Pollution: In rural areas, traditional cooking methods using solid fuels can contribute to indoor air pollution. Efforts are being made to promote cleaner cooking technologies to reduce indoor air pollution.
5. Monitoring Gaps: While air quality monitoring has improved, there are still gaps in the monitoring network, making it challenging to provide comprehensive real-time data on air quality.
Efforts are underway to address air pollution issues in Cambodia, with a focus on reducing emissions, promoting cleaner technologies, and improving air quality monitoring."
