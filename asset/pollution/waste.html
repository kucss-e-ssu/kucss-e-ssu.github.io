Waste

The waste issue is a global matter characterized by the continuous generation of waste, improper disposal leading to environmental degradation and health problems, wasteful use of valuable resources, and the exacerbation of social inequalities. To address this problem, effective waste management and recycling systems need to be established, public awareness should be raised, and sustainable consumption and production practices promoted.
Several factors contribute to this issue:
  • Inadequate waste collection and disposal infrastructure: Cambodia, especially in rural areas, faces challenges due to the lack of comprehensive waste collection and disposal infrastructure. Many local communities lack regular waste collection services, leading to improper waste disposal practices like open dumping and burning.
  • Increasing urbanization and waste generation: Rapid urbanization, particularly in cities like Phnom Penh, has led to a surge in waste generation. As more people migrate to urban areas, the quantity of solid waste in cities has significantly increased.
  • Plastic pollution: Plastic pollution has been a notable issue in Cambodia, with single-use plastics such as bags and packaging materials being widely used. These plastics often litter streets, rivers, and natural environments, causing visual pollution and posing threats to wildlife and aquatic ecosystems.
  • Health and environmental concerns: Improper waste management practices, such as outdoor burning and inadequate disposal, have posed health risks to local communities. Incinerating waste releases harmful pollutants into the air, and improper disposal can lead to soil and water contamination, impacting public health and the environment.
  • Lack of recycling and waste separation: Cambodia has limited recycling and waste separation programs. Most waste is either landfilled or openly burned, with a small fraction being recycled or composted.

How Can We Solve These Problems?

Recycling can reduce the amount of waste and save the materials used to make things. Also, you should use less disposable items. Using a garbage bag with the amount marked on it is also a way to reduce waste. Reducing trash can save money and reduce environmental pollution. Efforts are being made to address Cambodia's waste problem, including initiatives to improve waste collection and management infrastructure, promote recycling, and raise public awareness. NGOs and international organizations are also working with the Cambodian government to implement sustainable waste management practices. However, it remains a significant challenge that requires continued investment, education, and policy enforcement to mitigate its adverse effects on the environment and public health.
